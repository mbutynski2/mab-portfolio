FlightPro

Flight Attendant Tablet Application

Role
UX Designer

Timeframe
8 Weeks

Overview

An international aviation company would like a quick way for their flight attendants to track and record tasks. This app would enhance their existing tablet app for their flight crew. In my role as Senior UX, I led the discover, design, assisted development, and presented to stakeholders.

Goal

The goal was to create a proof of concept of an application in 8 weeks that would allow for flight attendants to quickly perform a checklist with the hopes that it would grow over time.

Process

Translation and communication

Due to language barriers, I enlisted the help of a colleague to translate the design thinking session materials from Spanish to English and recorded this meeting. Based on my previous history of working on an internal project, you never know who will be added/removed from your team at any moment. I created materials that could be easily transferred  to new teammates.

I asked each team member what they wanted to get out to of the project. This became a great ice-breaker to understand motivations and to be a support to help our teammates achieve their goals.

Discovery

I was given an InVision mockup. I wanted to incorporate, but not be restricted by the design, so I used InVision freehand to create rough wireframes that could be shared by the product/sales team to aid in our UX design discussions.

The scope changed after client meeting to integrate into an existing application. I requested screenshots of the current application so I could match the styles.

Design with Figma

Based on these decisions, I moved to low-fidelity wireframes using Figma. This is a collaboration layout program that allows for comments, as well as code view for developers of the CSS. You can also downloaded assets directly.

POC became clickable prototype

After this first week of focused UX design, the scope changed to feature this POC to the client and get feedback before writing code.

Mirroring the design on an actual IPad showed space issues between buttons and icons as  well as navigation challenges that needed  to be corrected.

I offered the client a closer experience by displaying the non-coded prototype on an actual tablet. By using the Figma mirroring app, anyone I share the prototype with could mirror the experience directly on the tablet.

Result

We presented the app and they were delighted by the interface. However, they questioned the idea of a checklist on a tablet based on real-world experience with the travel agents. From this experience, I took away a solid understanding of an end-to-end product developed at DXC.
